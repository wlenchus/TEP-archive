## The Luo derivation, restated


Stripping the physical apparatus, Luo's extremum condition is Eq. (8):


$$\omega\varepsilon_0 = \sigma\cos^2\theta_i \quad \text{with} \quad \cos\theta_i \ll 1$$


Everything else — the 82.8%, the 17.2%, the pseudo-Brewster, the vanishing skin depth — is consequence. The physics that matters is what sets this extremum.


In the good-conductor grazing limit, Luo arrives at the reflection (Eq. 10):


$$R = \frac{\omega^2\varepsilon_0^2 + \sigma^2\cos^4\theta_i}{(\omega\varepsilon_0 + \sqrt{2\sigma\omega\varepsilon_0}\cos\theta_i + \sigma\cos^2\theta_i)^2}$$


Define $q := \sigma\cos^2\theta_i/(\omega\varepsilon_0)$. This is the dimensionless ratio the whole derivation turns on. Then:


$$R = \frac{1 + q^2}{(1 + \sqrt{2q} + q)^2}$$


Minimize: $dR/dq = 0$ gives, after the algebra, $q = 1$. Substituting:


$$R_{\min} = \frac{2}{(2 + \sqrt{2})^2} = \frac{2}{6 + 4\sqrt{2}} = \frac{1}{3 + 2\sqrt{2}} = 3 - 2\sqrt{2}$$


$$A_{\max} = 1 - R_{\min} = 2\sqrt{2} - 2$$


So the extremum is: **$q = 1$, where $q = \sigma\cos^2\theta_i/(\omega\varepsilon_0)$.**


## Reading this through the TEP lens


Here is what jumps out, granting the framework's premise.


The quantity $q$ is a dimensionless ratio of two physical rates: $\sigma\cos^2\theta_i$ (effective conductivity current rate at angle $\theta_i$) versus $\omega\varepsilon_0$ (displacement current rate). In the standard EM framing these are the resistive and reactive currents, and their ratio is the loss tangent of the medium at the given geometry. In TEP vocabulary, these are exactly the **collinear (dissipative) and orthogonal (reactive) channels** of the ortholinear decomposition — with $\cos^2\theta_i$ as the geometric projection factor selecting how much of the current aligns with the tangential field.


The extremum condition $q = 1$ is then, in TEP's language, the **self-dual point of the ortholinear budget** — where the collinear and orthogonal channels are balanced. The reactive budget variable $x$ and the dissipative budget variable $u$ are equal, each carrying one unit of the coupling.


If I accept the framework's premise that self-dual points of hyperbolic budgets coincide with physical extrema, then $q = 1$ is *immediately* predicted as the extremum location, without needing to solve $dR/dq = 0$. The minimization is replaced by a structural identification. That is the parsimony claim in action.

## Candidate test: Kramers' barrier crossing at critical damping

$$m\ddot{x} + \eta\dot{x} = -V'(x) + \sqrt{2\eta k_B T}\,\xi(t)$$

$$\kappa(\eta) = \sqrt{1 + \left(\frac{\eta}{2m\omega_b}\right)^2} - \frac{\eta}{2m\omega_b}$$


This is a well-known, canonically derived result in chemical physics.

    omega_0 = np.sqrt(4 * a / m)          # well curvature frequency
