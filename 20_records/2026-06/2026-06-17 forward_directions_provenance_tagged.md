## 2. Direction A — Dynamics from a variational principle on the information geometry

**The gap.** The formalism recovers the *kinematic* skeleton of relativity (the group, the
hyperbolic geometry, the invariants) but not the *dynamical* content (equations of motion,
`c`-as-law, gravity-as-curvature). The framework's own foundational layer flags this floor as
the lone item separating "general organizing kinematics" from "physical theory" **[G, 6-15
manifold note §5]**.

**Where it attaches in the framework.** The raw material for dynamics is arguably already
present and merely not called dynamics:
- the cascade is a *flow* — a forgetting/heat semigroup **[C]** (this identification is from
  the summary; I did **not** re-read it as primary text this session — verify before
  relying);
- the purity speed limit is a *rate* statement, with an explicit saturating Hamiltonian
  `H_opt = iW/√F` and the rank-2 transport bivector `W = u∧v` **[G, 5-8 partial read]** — a
  speed limit is a constraint on dynamics, not kinematics;
- the α weak/strong-coupling exponent is a *rate* discriminator **[C]** (the specific
  numbers ≈0.914–0.917 and HEOM-testability are summary-sourced — verify).

**How it would proceed [P].** Lift the rate structure from inequalities to an equation of
motion: posit a variational principle (an action / least-dissipation functional) on the
monotone-metric geometry whose stationary trajectories *are* the physical dynamics, and whose
Euler–Lagrange content *selects* the physically-realized member of the admissible-dynamics
class that the kinematic floor only *classified*. Concretely: (i) write the purity/budget
flow as a gradient (or gradient-plus-Hamiltonian) flow of an information functional;
(ii) identify the functional and the metric whose gradient flow reproduces the known speed
limit as the saturating trajectory; (iii) check whether the same functional reproduces a
second, independent dynamical fact (so it is not just a re-encoding of the one bound).

**Why it crosses the gap.** A variational principle is what converts a *classification* of
admissible dynamics into a *selection* of the realized one — the precise thing the dynamical
floor lacks. If the physical equation of motion is the extremal of an information functional,
then dynamics is *derived from* the information geometry rather than imposed.

**Provenance and caveats.**
- **[X]** The mechanism I have in mind (dynamics as gradient/Hamiltonian flow on a metric
  structure) is the optimal-transport / GENERIC picture (JKO; Otto calculus; Öttinger) —
  *external* to TEP. This cuts against the corpus's stated preference for internal
  derivation, and I may be unaware that the framework already has an internal version of
  exactly this (the cascade-as-semigroup hint suggests it might). **First check: does the
  corpus already cast the cascade flow variationally?**
- **[C, T?]** The claim that the kinematic floor yields "classification not selection (the
  su(1,1) trichotomy)" is something I read only via the summary plus a second-hand reference,
  **not** from the 6-15 dynamical-floor note's body this session. The whole "lift
  classification to selection" framing depends on that being an accurate characterization —
  verify against the actual dynamical-floor note before building on it.
- This is the direction I would back most, *because* the rate material is already present —
  but that confidence is partly resting on **[C]** items (the semigroup, the α rates), so it
  is provisional until those are re-grounded.

## 3. Direction B — Closing the isomorphism→foundation gap

**The gap.** The framework shows information geometry *carries* the SU(1,1)/hyperbolic
structure of relativistic kinematics — a deep isomorphism. But that structure is ubiquitous
(squeezed light, the AGM, the Ising self-dual point, the Poincaré disk). A shared skeleton
does not establish that physics is *grounded in* information rather than *parallel to* it; the
strong claim is directional, and an isomorphism does not fix direction.

**Two sub-routes, of different character.**

**B1 — Fill the ledger (the empirical route) [G framework item, C specifics].** The
pre-registration program (O-k) is already specified in the corpus: define the class signature
(two-channel √2-cross-coupled balance; the `z²−2√2z+1` / rate-saturation crossing),
enumerate unmeasured domains, pre-register the γ* = 2−√2 appearance **[G, 6-9 III O-k]**. The
live, unmeasured, could-have-failed candidate is the α-discriminator — a ~0.3% gap between
the linear budget coordinate and the curved entropy fixed point, carried forward as the one
genuinely open falsifiable early prediction **[C, summary/6-7 synthesis]** (the magnitude and
HEOM-accessibility are summary-sourced — verify). *How:* run or commission that measurement
(or another pre-registered domain), with the class signature timestamped first. *Why it
crosses:* a could-have-failed prediction confirmed *with priority* is exactly what converts
"shares the structure" into "predicts in the structure" — the thing the Liu episode showed
missing **[G, 6-9 II.1]**.

**B2 — Prove uniqueness (the forcing route) [P].** Strengthen the framework's existing
*modulo* clauses into a uniqueness theorem: show that the information-geometric reconstruction
is the *only* one — that any system meeting the measurement/verification axioms *must* have
the Lorentzian information geometry, and conversely that the physical content is *fixed by*
it. This is continuous with the forcing arguments the corpus already runs — the budget forced
modulo measure-completeness + 2-integrability **[G, 6-9 I.2 for the L²/2-integrability clause;
C for the "measure-completeness" framing]**, SU(1,1) forced for flux-conserving cascades **[C,
5-30 §3]**. *How:* discharge the verificationist base (show it is forced, not a posit), then
show no competing isomorphic structure yields the same physics from the same axioms. *Why it
crosses:* uniqueness is what licenses the directional "physics emerges from information"
claim — without it, the isomorphism is symmetric and the arrow is unargued.

**Provenance and caveats.**
- **[C]** Much of B1's force rests on the α-discriminator being the live candidate at the
  stated magnitude with the stated test — all summary-sourced. If those are stale, B1's
  concreteness weakens (though the O-k *program* is grounded).
- **[P, ?]** B2 is my framing. I have **not** surveyed whether the corpus already attempts a
  uniqueness/forcing result of this kind (the O-a transitivity discharge **[G, 6-15]** and
  the budget-forcing arguments are in this family, so a uniqueness result may be closer than I
  know, or may already exist in documents I have not read).
- **[C]** "SU(1,1) forced for flux-conserving cascades, conditional on indefinite flux" is my
  recollection of the 5-30 result via the summary; the conditionality is the crux of B2 and
  must be re-grounded from 5-30 directly.

## 4. Direction C — A metric commitment for physical contact (with a real tension flagged)

**The gap.** The framework is deliberately metric-agnostic; the physical metric at `d≥3` is
unselected (the corpus's "where the genuine open physics lives"). Sharp physical predictions
generally require committing to a specific metric. The generality and the predictive thinness
are the same feature.

**Where it attaches.** The selection is half-supplied: Bures/SLD = QFI is the *unique*
two-point-homogeneous (constant-curvature) member of the monotone family — verified on the
qubit slice, K constant for Bures and varying for Kubo–Mori/RLD **[G, 6-15 note §3 + the
appended computation]**. So there is a principled distinguished member already on the table.

**How it would proceed [P].** Argue that a *physical* requirement forces the symmetric member
where physics needs a metric: e.g. optimal parameter estimation (Braunstein–Caves QFI), or
the data-processing-saturating / reversibility condition, selects Bures/QFI. That converts
metric-agnosticism into a *context-conditioned commitment* and unlocks sharp predictions.

**Why it crosses.** A metric commitment is the missing ingredient for moving from structural
recognition to quantitative prediction in a given physical setting.

**The tension I have to flag honestly [T?].** Direction C, as first stated ("select *the*
metric"), risks misreading the framework. The 6-15 note is explicit that the
metric-multiplicity is **not** "a defect awaiting a selection rule" — it is the *shape
leverage*, the very thing that gives the framework its `d≥3` content **[G, 6-15 §3 item 2]**.
So a path that *collapses* the family would cut against a load-bearing framework stance. The
reconciliation — and the corrected form of the direction — is: keep the full family as the
*foundational* object (the shape axis), and treat metric selection as a **per-physical-context
identification** (which member describes *this* system's distinguishability), not a global
collapse. Whether that distinction is what the framework intends, or whether even
context-selection is seen as against the spirit of metric-agnosticism, is something I cannot
settle from what I have read — it needs the corpus's own treatment of O-8/O-C, which I have
seen only in summary form **[C]** and via the closing note's one-line framing **[G,
5-29 closing note]**. **This is the clearest instance in the note of incomplete framework
grounding distorting a direction**, and it is recorded as such rather than smoothed over.
