## Proven results

### Result A: Universal product law (replaces v1's chain-specific claim)

For an N-DHO system with **any graph topology** of symmetric coupling
(path, cycle, star, complete, tree, or arbitrary), uniform natural
frequency ω_n, and any (possibly non-uniform) damping ratios:

Π_j ω_j = ω_n\^N · √det(I − K)

where ω_j are the lossless normal mode frequencies and K is the
symmetric coupling matrix (K_ij = κ_ij, K_ii = 0). This was verified to
machine precision across path, cycle, star, complete-K_4, Y-tree, and
caterpillar graphs.

### Result B: Chain-specific zeta-invariant for cross-transfer zeros

For an N-DHO **chain** with same ω_n, the zeros of Re(H\_{1,N}(ω)) (the
cross-transfer between the two end ports) satisfy:

Π_k ω_k\^{(zero)} = ω_n\^N · √det(I − K_jacobi), exactly, ζ-independent

where K_jacobi is the symmetric tridiagonal Jacobi coupling matrix.

This is **specific to chains** with end-to-end transfer. For other
topologies the cross-transfer between two ports sees a subset of modes
(those whose eigenvectors couple those two ports), so the simple
product-of-zeros law does not directly extend; one must instead use the
universal Result A on all normal mode frequencies.

This corrects an overgeneralization in v1.

### Result C: Boost law for the 3-chain (uniform damping)

For a 3-DHO chain with uniform damping ratio ζ and chain couplings (κ_1,
κ_2):

cosh(2 α_eff) = (1 + 6 ζ²) · G(K), K = √(κ_1² + κ_2²)

where α_eff is the rapidity (1/2)ln(ω\_+/ω\_-) extracted from the
outermost reactive crossings (after factoring the protected ω = ω_n
mode). The prefactor (1 + 6ζ²) is cosh(2α_0), the zero-coupling
rapidity. This generalizes the 2-DHO boost law cosh(2α_eff) =
G(κ)cosh(2α_0).

Verified to machine precision. Note: this requires uniform damping;
non-uniform damping breaks this clean form.

### Result D: Boost law breaks at N = 4

For the 4-DHO chain with uniform damping, the analog of the boost law
does **not** hold cleanly. The reactive crossing polynomial factors at ζ
= 0 into two quadratics with golden-ratio-related effective couplings
(`1 ± φκ` and `1 ± κ/φ`), but finite ζ breaks this clean factorization.
The pair products drift with ζ, indicating the 2- and 3-chain boost laws
are exceptional cases.

### Result E: Path-Jacobi spectrum and the metallic-mean appearance

For an N-DHO chain at ζ = 0 with uniform coupling κ_0, the normal mode
frequencies satisfy:

ω_j² = ω_n² · (1 − κ_0 · λ_j), λ_j = 2 cos(jπ/(N+1)), j = 1, ..., N

These eigenvalues exhibit specific arithmetic structure:

N = 2: λ ∈ {+1, −1} N = 3: λ ∈ {√2, 0, −√2} (includes zero → mode at
ω_n) N = 4: λ ∈ {φ, 1/φ, −1/φ, −φ} (golden ratio) N = 5: λ ∈ {√3, 1, 0,
−1, −√3} (includes zero → mode at ω_n) N = 6: λ_j = 2cos(jπ/7), with
relation to silver and bronze means

This identifies the framework's "metallic mean hierarchy" (a Tier 4
structural observation) with the path-Jacobi Chebyshev spectrum --- a
standard object in algebraic combinatorics. The framework is correctly
identifying this structure; it has a known mathematical home.

For odd N, the spectrum includes λ = 0, giving a protected mode at ω =
ω_n. This is why the 3-chain factorization (Result C) works cleanly: the
protected mode factors out, leaving a 2-DHO-like residual.

## Connection to pendulum chains (linear regime)

A chain of pendulums coupled by torsion springs, linearized around θ =
0, satisfies:

θ_k'' + 2ζω_n θ_k' + ω_n² θ_k = ω_n² κ \[(θ\_{k-1} − θ_k) + (θ\_{k+1} −
θ_k)\]

This is a **graph Laplacian** coupling, not the off-diagonal-only Jacobi
coupling of the DHO investigation. The path-N Laplacian eigenvalues are:

μ_j = 4 sin²(jπ/(2N)), j = 0, 1, ..., N−1

These also have metallic structure: for N = 5, μ ∈ {0, 2−φ, 2−1/φ,
2+1/φ, 2+φ}.

So pendulum chains inherit the same structural family of mode spectra,
just with the spectrum shifted (μ_j = 2 − 2cos(jπ/N) instead of
2cos(jπ/(N+1))). The framework's structural predictions extend to
pendulum chains in the linearized regime, with appropriate translation.

## Speculative: pendulum-chain chaos onset and the sinc² budget

**This section is observational and not yet rigorous.**

Each pendulum has a natural budget structure:

x²(θ) = sin²(θ) / θ² = sinc²(θ) u(θ) = 1 − sinc²(θ)

This compares the actual nonlinear restoring force `sin(θ)` to its
linear proxy `θ`. The self-dual point x² = 1/2 occurs at θ ≈ 79.7°.

**Observation:** For an N-pendulum chain (N = 5, κ = 0.3) initialized in
mode 1 with various amplitudes, mode-1 energy stays trapped (\>95%) at
θ_max ≤ 70° and starts leaking significantly by θ_max = 90°. The
transition brackets the sinc²-budget self-dual point.

**Caveats this observation requires:**

1.  The match works for N = 5. For N = 4 the leak onset is offset by
    \~10° (transition near 90° rather than 80°). For N = 3 mode 1 stays
    trapped at all amplitudes I tested, indicating symmetry-protection
    that the sinc² budget does not capture.

2.  Different couplings κ shift the threshold: at fixed N = 5, κ = 0.1
    gives earlier leak onset and κ = 0.6 gives later. The sinc²
    threshold is coupling-independent, so this isn't a complete story.

3.  The characteristic chaos onset in coupled-oscillator systems is
    governed by the standard FPU criterion (nonlinearity vs mode gap),
    which gives amplitude scales depending on κ and N. The sinc²
    self-dual point is *one* characteristic scale among several.

**What is genuinely interesting:** the sinc² budget is exactly the kind
of TEP-style "x² + u = 1" budget the framework predicts for any
oscillator with nonlinear restoring force --- and its self-dual point
falls in the correct neighborhood for chaos onset in some
configurations. Whether this sharpens to a quantitative prediction
depends on more careful analysis, including:

-   Mode-resolved budget (each mode has its own sinc²-like quantity from
    projection of full nonlinear potential onto that mode's coordinate)
-   Comparison to standard FPU thresholds
-   Behavior on the homoclinic orbit / KAM threshold

I would not at this point claim the framework gives a "less inscrutable"
treatment of chaos. What it gives is a candidate **local coordinate**
--- the sinc² budget on each pendulum --- that has the right qualitative
scale. Sharpening it to a quantitative criterion is open.

## Implications for the framework

**Strengthens:** The det(I−K) law and metallic-mean spectrum
identification give the framework's "metallic hierarchy" a precise
mathematical home in Chebyshev / orthogonal polynomial theory. This is
structural enrichment.

**Refines:** v1's claim that "the Pythagorean form is exact only at N ≤
3" needs further qualification. The Pythagorean form holds exactly for N
= 2, exactly for N-stars (any N, by rank-2 structure), and at low order
for chains. The general law is the matching polynomial of the coupling
graph.

**Extends:** The boost law `cosh(2α_eff) = G(κ)cosh(2α_0)` extends
exactly to the 3-chain (with K = √(Σκ²) and prefactor (1+6ζ²)) but not
to N ≥ 4 chains in clean closed form.

**Open:** A clean N-chain boost law in closed form. Conjecture: it
should have a determinant or matching-polynomial form, but I haven't
found it.

**Open:** A rigorous TEP-budget criterion for chaos onset in coupled
nonlinear systems. The sinc² self-dual point is a candidate local
coordinate but is not the full story.
