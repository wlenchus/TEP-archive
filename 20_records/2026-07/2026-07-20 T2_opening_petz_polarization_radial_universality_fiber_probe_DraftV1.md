## 2. The martingale, demystified [V] — and Will's construction verified

The confusion is legitimate and the resolution is clean: **the randomness in the polarization martingale is synthetic.** There is no stochastic environment — the cascade is deterministic; the "random variable" is a uniformly chosen *address* (branch sequence) in the deterministic tree. The martingale is a counting device: boundedness ⟹ a.s. convergence; conservation ⟹ the mean is pinned; the contraction rung ⟹ the only stable limits are the poles; therefore the *fraction of addresses* at each pole is pinned. Nothing incompressible is being compressed — **incompressibility is being concentrated**: the u-fraction of addresses becomes pure noise, the x²-fraction becomes pure signal, and the budget identity becomes exact at the level of address-fractions. "Randomness is incompressible" is the u-pole *of* the theorem, not an objection to it. (This is also the precise sense in which the method is a measure→pointwise bridge: martingale convergence upgrades a conserved average to an almost-every-address statement — the relevance to (A)-type problems, and also its limit: "almost every address" is not "every integer.")
