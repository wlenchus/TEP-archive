## 0. The claim, and the register that was wrong

**The claim.** A derivative is the comparison of two continua. A single budget supplies one. The
second is not borrowed from a second *state* (that route is flat and trivial) but **lifted**: a
path along one dimension that is too rough to transport as a scalar is carried instead by a
second, circumventional degree of freedom, where the obstruction to its smoothness reappears as
**holonomy**. That holonomy is the **Lévy area** — the level-2 datum of a rough path — and its
magnitude is the path's **fractal dimension / roughness**. "Memory," "lag," "inertia," and
"frame-dragging" are all this one object, read without the temporal vocabulary that made them
look dimensionful.

**The register that was wrong.** The session first proposed the dynamics was a *metriplectic
(GENERIC) flow* generated by a single capacity functional. That was retracted on three counts,
all of which this thread inverts: (i) it lived in the dimensionally-rooted instantiation (qubit
purity, QFI, Mandelstam–Tamm, the Hamiltonian) that cannot yield an agnostic form; (ii) it
generated the whole dynamics from *one scalar*, which the corpus had already shown trivializes a
derivative (a single scalar tie removes one of the two degrees of freedom — 5-21 §6.4); and
(iii) a scalar potential's connection is **exact/flat**, so it cannot carry the holonomy that the
dynamics actually is. The correct object is a *connection*, and its content is in the
**non-abelian frame**, never in a scalar reduction of it.

## 1. The one-clock obstruction [G, 5-21]

A single two-term budget `x²+u=1` cannot host a nontrivial derivative: the sector ratio is locked
(`d(x²)/du = −1`), so "the dynamics lives in the clock `dη/dt`," which a single budget does not
fix. A genuine derivative needs *two* clocks — and crucially, **no single scalar tie between them
will do**: any scalar conversion removes one degree of freedom and trivializes the comparison
(5-21 §6.4). The two clocks must be related by *transport*, not by a function.

This is also why the underdetermination worry (the geometry does not fix a Hamiltonian) is not the
interesting obstruction. It is true and elementary; the framework's own audit already logged it
(6-7 III.5: the conditional-vs-unconditional drift; "the bare budget does not demand a metric").
The interesting content is not *which* second clock, but *what kind of object* the second clock is.

So the two routes are not two options of equal standing: **a scalar second clock is always flat;
a genuine second clock is a lift to the non-abelian frame.** This is the structural reason the

## 5. The adversarial resolution: memory is non-commutativity, not dimensionful time [V, this session]

The decisive objection: if the second clock is the reservoir's *memory* of the signal's past, then
dimensionful time has re-entered and the signal is privileged as the parameter time runs along.
The session's temporal vocabulary ("lag," "history," "remembers") *was* that smuggling. The actual
object is not.

Curvature is `[∇_a, ∇_b]` — the failure of the two clocks to **commute**. What a closed loop
measures is `(a-then-b) − (b-then-a)`: **order-dependence, not past-dependence**. This needs no
time — commutators are kinematic. Three checks [V, this session]:

- **Dimensionless.** The curvature is `dη ∧ dη`, an oriented *area* of two rapidities — a pure
  number. The holonomy `∮A = ∬F` is a dimensionless oriented count.
- **Agnostic.** It is the non-commutativity of *any* two clocks — the framework's own **t-axis**
  (associativity, `[f_a,f_b]=O(t)`), dimensionless and instantiation-blind by construction.
- **No privileged slot.** In symmetric gauge `F ∝ dΣ∧dΔ`; under the swap `a↔b` (Σ→Σ, Δ→−Δ),
  `F → −F`. The curvature is **swap-odd**, a Δ-sector quantity, exactly like `Δ`, `δ`, `x_a−x_b`
  in 5-21. Its magnitude is swap-symmetric; only its sign (the handedness of the holonomy) flips,
  which is convention up to the swap reflection. No slot is privileged.

The clean separation: **temporal memory (σ-axis, past-weighting) would be dimensionful and
privileging — and it is *not* what the curvature is.** The curvature is **t-axis
non-commutativity (order-dependence)**, which is timeless. The static state being unreachable now
reads correctly: it is the limit where the two clocks *commute* (flat, zero area), which collapses
the second clock and with it the derivative — staticity is the absence of dynamics, not a state
within it.
