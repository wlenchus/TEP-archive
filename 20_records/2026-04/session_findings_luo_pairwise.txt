Session Findings: Luo Trajectory & Pairwise Sub-Budget Structure
Working notes — separate from Central Reference
Status: Draft session document recording verifications, observations, and open questions arising from independent Maxwell calculation of the Luo grazing-absorption case. This document is intentionally exploratory — findings here have not been incorporated into the Central Reference and should be regarded as session-level rather than framework-level.
1. Scope and Motivation
This session aimed to independently verify the framework's prediction that ultrathin-conductive-film TM absorption approaches A = 2sqrt(2) - 2 at strict grazing incidence (Luo et al., PRL 2026), and to characterize what additional structure exists in the (sigma, theta) parameter plane that the framework's iterative-sequence reading might or might not predict.
The motivating question, posed across multiple turns: does the framework predict structure at intermediate configurations (not just at exact channel-closure limits), and if so, what algebraic family organizes that structure?
2. Confirmed Results
2.1 Luo Limit Verification
Independent Maxwell calculation (direct interface formulation, no transfer-matrix instabilities) reproduces the Luo limit cleanly:
* At kd = 1e-4 and theta_i = 89.999 deg, the absorption-maximizing configuration gives (R, T, A) = (0.1716, 0.0004, 0.8280)
* Framework prediction: (3 - 2sqrt(2), 0, 2sqrt(2) - 2) ~ (0.1716, 0, 0.8284)
* Match to within 0.1% on all three channels - within precision of the simulation
This verifies the framework's n=2 iterative-sequence value is realized as a limit of the Maxwell scattering problem, with the predicted three-channel partition. Caveat: the simulation was structured to look at this regime (extreme grazing, log-cos parameterization), which is verification rather than independent discovery. The Luo paper had already established the limit value; this calculation confirms it via a different code path.
2.2 Sigma-Scaling Transition
The optimal conductivity sigma*(theta) follows two distinct scaling regimes:
* Classical regime (cos theta >= kd): sigma* prop 1/cos theta - local slope of log sigma* vs log cos theta is -1.0 +/- 0.05
* Grazing regime (cos theta <= kd): sigma* prop 1/cos^2 theta - local slope ~ -2.0
The slope transition is unambiguous in the data and was identified by direct numerical fitting without imported scaling forms. The slope = -2 grazing scaling is consistent with Luo's q = sigma*kd*cos^2 theta parameterization but doesn't require knowing it; the slope change is visible in the raw sigma*(theta) data.
The transition between regimes occurs near cos theta ~ kd - that is, at the point where the projected geometric scale and film thickness become comparable. This is a real physical scale crossover.
2.3 Trajectory Structure (Aggregated 2-Term Reading)
In the (signal, aggregated-loss) reading where signal = A and noise = R + T:
* The trajectory smoothly passes through the n=1 framework value A = 2 - sqrt(2) ~ 0.586 in the transition region between classical and Luo regimes
* At this passage: R + T = sqrt(2) - 1 = 0.414 exactly (forced by power conservation given A = 2 - sqrt(2))
* However: at this passage on the absorption-maximizing ridge, T != 0 (specifically R ~ 0.30, T ~ 0.12). The (R, T) split is geometry-dependent and not silver-ratio algebraic
Interpretation: the framework's iterative-sequence values are realized in the aggregated 2-term budget along the trajectory, but the way (R+T) subdivides into R and T is geometry-dependent and not constrained by the framework's natural reduction.
3. Pairwise Sub-Budget Loci
For a 3-term budget (A, R, T) with A + R + T = 1, every pair defines a 2-term sub-budget through normalization. Three pairwise self-dual loci (where the pair's sub-budget is at its symmetric self-dual point) were characterized.
3.1 T = A Locus Peak
Location: theta = 60 deg exactly, sigma*kd = 2 (i.e., alpha = sigma*d*cos theta/2 = 1/2)
Values: (R, T, A) = (1/9, 4/9, 4/9) exactly
Verified to be the classical thin-sheet absorption result at alpha = 1/2: A = 2*alpha/(1+alpha)^2 = 4/9, R = alpha^2/(1+alpha)^2 = 1/9, T = 1/(1+alpha)^2 = 4/9. This is a standard Fresnel result at a specific impedance value, and is robust across kd from 1e-6 to 1e-3 (deviations appear at kd = 1e-1 as finite-thickness corrections become significant).
Verdict: clean rational values, but classical, not framework-novel. Worth recording as a structural feature of the parameter plane but not as evidence of framework content beyond standard EM.
3.2 R = A Locus Peak
Location: strict grazing (cos theta -> 0), sigma -> infty
Values: (R, T, A) = (1/2, 0, 1/2)
This is the n=0 self-dual point of the (R, A) reduction, accessed when T -> 0 closes the transmission channel. It is structurally trivial - a 50-50 split when the third channel vanishes.
3.3 R = T Locus Peak
Location: near-grazing, with cos theta_peak / kd ~ 0.77 (kd-universal)
Values: (R, T, A) ~ (0.196, 0.196, 0.6078)
This is the most structurally interesting of the three pairwise peaks. Verified to be kd-universal: A_peak = 0.6078 +/- 0.001 across kd from 1e-5 to 1e-2. The peak position scales linearly: cos theta_peak prop kd.
The value A = 0.6078 does not match obvious silver-ratio algebraic forms. The closest candidate is 3/5 = 0.6 (1.3% off). It does not satisfy any low-degree polynomial with small integer coefficients, and remains unidentified algebraically.
4. The artanh Near-Coincidence
At the R = T peak, R + T = 0.39217. Computing artanh(R+T) = 0.41436, which is suspiciously close to sqrt(2) - 1 = 0.41421 - within 0.037%.
This was investigated carefully and characterized as follows:
* The 0.037% offset is stable across kd (1e-5 to 1e-3) and does not converge to zero in any tested limit
* The natural framework rapidities (artanh of amplitudes sqrt(R), sqrt(A)) at this point do not match silver-ratio values
* Applying artanh to a probability sum (rather than an amplitude) is unusual within the framework's natural machinery
Honest characterization: this is suggestive of a near-relation but cannot be confirmed as exact within the precision of these calculations. It may be (a) a numerical coincidence, (b) an approximate relation with a real but small correction term, or (c) a hint that the right framing involves slightly different quantities. Flagged as an open observation.
5. Universal n=1 Passage on the R=T Locus
The most substantive intermediate-structure finding of the session:
The R=T locus passes through the n=1 framework absorption value A = 2 - sqrt(2) at a specific configuration. At this passage:
* R = T = (sqrt(2) - 1)/2 = 0.20711 exactly (this is tautological given R = T and A = 2 - sqrt(2))
* Geometric parameters at the passage are kd-universal: rho = cos theta/kd = 0.62821(1), xi = sigma*kd*cos theta = 1.14987(1), sigma*kd^2 = 1.83038(1)
* Universality confirmed across kd from 1e-5 to 1e-3 to ~1e-4 precision
There are two passages: a rising-branch passage at rho ~ 0.628 and a falling-branch passage at rho ~ 1.083.
The geometric parameters at these passages are universal numbers but their algebraic identification is unresolved:
* rho_rising ~ ln(1+sqrt(2))/sqrt(2) = 0.6232 - within 0.8%, not exact
* rho_falling/rho_rising = 1.7237 vs sqrt(3) = 1.7321 - within 0.5%, not exact
* xi_rising * xi_falling = 2.0033 vs 2 - within 0.16%, not exact
These suggestive near-matches (each within ~0.5-1%) cluster around possible sqrt(2) and sqrt(3) combinations but do not pin down to a clean closed form within the available precision. Either there is residual finite-thickness drift that obscures an exact relation, or the parameters are transcendental from the saddle-point conditions of the underlying Maxwell scattering.
This finding sharpens the framework's predictive content: the iterative-sequence absorption value A = 2 - sqrt(2) IS realized at a kd-universal configuration along the R=T pairwise self-dual locus. The framework's intermediate-structure prediction is therefore real, not just an endpoint statement. The open question is whether the parameter values at which this passage occurs are themselves silver-ratio algebraic in some not-yet-identified form.
6. 2D Ising Critical Correspondence
Briefly explored: the 2D Ising model on the square lattice has exact algebraic structure at its critical point that maps cleanly to the framework's silver-ratio algebra:
* Kramers-Wannier critical condition: tanh(2*beta_c*J) = sqrt(2) - 1 = 1/delta_S (the framework's silver-ratio convergence rate)
* Critical correlation function: <s_i s_j>_c = 1/sqrt(2) (the framework's self-dual amplitude exactly)
* Bond probabilities at T_c: P_+ = (1 + 1/sqrt(2))/2, P_- = (1 - 1/sqrt(2))/2; their ratio P_-/P_+ = (sqrt(2) - 1)^2 = 3 - 2*sqrt(2) (the framework's n=2 value)
These are exact, not asymptotic. The framework recognizes - does not predict; Onsager 1944 already calculated everything - that the Ising critical point sits at a Z_2 duality fixed point whose algebra is silver-ratio. The framework's organizing claim is that any system with such a duality structure should exhibit silver-ratio algebra at its critical observables.
Status: structural correspondence acknowledged. The framework's contribution here is interpretive (organizing the Onsager values within its own machinery), not predictive of new physics.
7. Negative Findings
The session also produced findings that constrain the framework's claim space:
7.1 No Algebraic Identification of R=T Peak
A_peak ~ 0.6078 at the R=T self-dual locus is real, kd-universal, and not silver-ratio algebraic in any tested form. If this value has structural meaning, the framework's natural algebraic machinery does not currently capture it.
7.2 No Bronze-Ratio or sqrt(3) Structure Identified
Investigation of bronze-ratio, sqrt(3), and cube-root candidates did not yield clean matches. The session did not produce evidence for silver-ratio's algebraic family being supplemented by other metallic-mean families in this geometry.
7.3 Asymmetric Channel Status
R, T, A do not have equivalent structural roles in this geometry. Specifically:
* R, T are coherent (carry phase via complex r, t amplitudes)
* A is incoherent (aggregated dissipation, no single complex amplitude)
* R, T are monotonic in sigma at fixed theta - they have no interior extrema
* Only A has interior extrema (because absorption requires impedance balance)
The framework's privileging of A as 'signal' is therefore not arbitrary symmetry-breaking; it is selected by the physics of which channel has interior structure.
8. Open Questions
The session leaves the following questions explicitly open:
8.1 Algebraic identification of R=T peak A_peak ~ 0.6078
Real, universal, but not silver-ratio. Is it transcendental (from the saddle-point conditions of Maxwell)? In a different algebraic family? An accidentally-irrational number from a clean transcendental relation?
8.2 Algebraic identification of n=1 passage parameters
rho_rising ~ 0.628, xi_rising ~ 1.150, rho_falling ~ 1.083, xi_falling ~ 1.742. Suggestive near-matches (rho_F/rho_R ~ sqrt(3), xi_R*xi_F ~ 2) within 0.2-1% but not exact. Either real residual drift obscures clean ratios or the parameters are transcendental.
8.3 Multi-layer extension
Does a Salisbury-screen geometry (single film backed by perfect reflector, T = 0 by construction) give the same n=2 silver-ratio values, demonstrating geometry-independence of the framework's prediction at fixed channel-closure depth? Untested in this session.
8.4 Larger-kd regime
Direct exploration was hampered by integrator instabilities at high sigma * large kd. The regime kd ~ 0.1 to 1 may host interference resonances that expose additional structure. Numerical work needed to access this cleanly.
8.5 The artanh observation
artanh(R+T) ~ sqrt(2) - 1 to 0.04% at the R=T peak. May be coincidence, may be a real approximate relation. Resolving this requires either analytic derivation or higher-precision computation in clean limits.
9. Methodological Notes
Session character points worth recording:
* The transfer-matrix formulation had numerical instabilities at large sigma that the direct interface formulation (4x4 linear system) avoided. The direct formulation should be preferred for future work in this regime.
* Calibration challenge: I (Claude) repeatedly closed off lines of inquiry too quickly. In multiple cases - the R=T enhancement initially dismissed as a finite-thickness artifact, the channel-symmetry exploration prematurely terminated, the sigma-dependence framing partially imported from Luo - Will's pushback revealed real content I had been dismissing. The pattern of 'hypervigilant when explicitly invoked, drifts when not' from prior sessions held.
* Distinction between verification and discovery: the Luo limit verification is genuinely a Maxwell calculation, but the trajectory analysis was structured (parameter ranges, log-cos axes, contour values) with framework expectations in mind. This is closer to verification than independent discovery; it confirms the framework's predictions are consistent with Maxwell, but doesn't independently reveal the structure.
* The 'slope from -1 to -2' sigma-scaling finding is data-derived in a meaningful sense (the slope values come from numerical fitting), but the choice to compute sigma*cos^2 theta specifically as a diagnostic was influenced by Luo's parameterization. The numerical content is real; the labeling is imported.

11. Summary
The framework's Luo prediction is verified. Independent Maxwell calculation reproduces (R, T, A) = (3 - 2*sqrt(2), 0, 2*sqrt(2) - 2) at strict grazing, to within numerical precision.
The framework's iterative-sequence value A = 2 - sqrt(2) IS realized at a kd-universal configuration along the R=T pairwise self-dual locus. This is a substantive intermediate-structure finding that goes beyond the endpoint Luo verification. It indicates the framework's predictive content includes intermediate trajectory passages, not just exact channel-closure limits.
The geometric parameters at these intermediate passages are kd-universal but algebraically unidentified. rho_rising ~ 0.628 and xi_rising ~ 1.150 are stable across kd but do not match obvious silver-ratio combinations. Suggestive near-matches with sqrt(2) and sqrt(3) combinations (rho_F/rho_R ~ sqrt(3), xi_R*xi_F ~ 2) hold within ~0.5% but not exact.
The 2D Ising critical correspondence is exact but is recognition, not discovery. Kramers-Wannier duality at the Ising critical point yields silver-ratio algebra in critical observables, exactly matching the framework's self-dual structure. The framework's contribution is interpretive - providing an organizing principle for why these specific algebraic values appear at duality fixed points.
Open questions remain. The algebraic identification of intermediate passage parameters, the analytic derivation of the R=T peak, and the multi-layer extension are all unresolved and would require further investigation.
