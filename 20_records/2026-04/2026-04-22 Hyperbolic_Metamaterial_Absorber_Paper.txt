ABSTRACT
We propose a biaxial hyperbolic metamaterials (HMMs) sawtooth absorber using anisotropic black phosphorus
(BP) and demonstrate that it can achieve a topological transition from an elliptical dispersion to a hyperbolic
dispersion. We also show the material to be an epsilon-near-zero HMM and validate an effective medium theory.
Due to    anisotropic permittivity and light confinement, our absorber achieves an average absorption of up to
93.4%, omnidirectionality (up to   ), and polarization sensitivity from 30 to    We gain physical insights
about the high absorption using electric field distributions and absorbed power distribution. Unlike the physical
mechanism of common multilayer metal/dielectric sawtooth absorbers, which is related to slow-light effect, the
absorption of our proposed absorber is attributed to the localized surface plasmon resonance, plasmon polar-
itons. Our proposed HMM can be applied in the infrared to terahertz region and we conclude by providing
practical guidelines for future research on biaxial hyperbolic metamaterials and anisotropic two-dimensional
materials.
Introduction
Natural materials represent only part of the theoretical electromag-
netic model. Metamaterials (MMs), which are specially designed mate-
rials that allow strong electric-field localization and light confinement at
subwavelength levels owing to light-matter interactions, have been
used for sub-diffraction-limited images [1] the fabrication of special
lenses [2] electromagnetic stealth [3,4] color printing applications [5]
and perfect absorption [6-10]. Metamaterial absorbers are an important
developing branch of MMs and have attracted substantial research
attention due to their various applications, which include radiative
cooling [11] thermal energy devices [12] color filters [13] selective
thermal emitters [14] and bio-sensing [15]. Moghimi et al. proposed an
infrared broadband absorber for an infrared stealth device [16]. Wu
et al. presented an Earth's atmospheric transparency window absorber
for radiative cooling [17]. Lin et al. proposed a solar absorber that can
achieve efficient photothermal conversion and rapidly heat up to 160°
under solar radiation [18]. However, design challenges increase signif-
icantly when it comes to infrared (IR) absorbers because the plasmonic
characteristics of metal-based configurations deteriorate rapidly for long
wavelengths [19]. Recently, Wang et al. have constructed many valu-
able terahertz (THz) absorbers through various metamaterial arrays
[20-29]. In addition, Guo et al. proposed some ultra-broadband, wide-
angle Thz perfect absorber [30,31]. Although metal is the most common
conductive building block in MAs, it has crucial disadvantages, such as
excessive thickness, narrow absorption range, and limited adjustment
capability.
Two-dimensional materials (2DMs) are useful constituents, usually
comprising a conducting sheet of single-atom thickness that can be
dominated by plasmons and dynamically tuned by electrostatic gating
[32-34]. Since graphene was isolated by the Manchester group in 2004,
2DMs consisting of a monolayer or a small number of layers of atoms
have been extensively studied because of their unusual photonic and
electronic properties [35]. 2DMs can present plasmon polaritons,
phonon polaritons, and even new hybrid plasmon-phonon-polaritons
[36].
Recently, the novel 2DM black phosphorus (BP) has attracted
research attention due to its superior performance, initiating a new

aging and cancer treatment research [37,38]. Theoretical and
experimental research has shown that BP supports localized surface
plasmon resonances in the IR to THz spectrum, which can enhance ab-

absorbers based on BP have similar shortcomings: low efficiency, single
absorption frequency, and narrow working bandwidth, which severely
limit their industrial applications in the IR to THz region. This is mainly
because the intensity of the plasmon resonances in a BP monolayer is
quite weak. THz waves have frequencies from 0.1 to 10 THz (30 to 3000
µm) and are widely applied in spectroscopy, medical imaging, modula-
tors, security, and communication technology [43-46]. But to date, it
has proven challenging to design an ultra-broadband THz absorber using
BP.
An intriguing class of MMs is hyperbolic metamaterials (HMMs), so
named because of their hyperbolic equifrequency wavevector disper-
sion, caused by their highly anisotropic features. The resulting hyper-
bolic equifrequent dispersion curve leads to dramatic changes in the
materials light propagation behaviors [32,47]. HMMs are capable of
limiting the electromagnetic field in a very small space while at the same
time allowing it to propagate in the form of volume plasmon polaritons
(VPPs), which MMs cannot do [48]. HMMs with near-zero parameters
are an intriguing and important class and have been extensively studied;
these include epsilon-near-zero (ENZ) HMMs, with permittivity near
zero; mu-near-zero HMMs, with permeability near zero; and index-near-
zero HMMs, with both permittivity and permeability near zero [49].
HMMs have demonstrated many promising features, such as broad-
band and omnidirectional optical absorption, the ability to control
leaky-wave radiation, and applicability in hot-electric devices, super-
lenses, and spontaneous emission enhancement [50-52]. In homoge-
nous natural hyperbolic materials, the working wavelengths are always
fixed. For example, the hyperbolic properties of the crystal structure of
  

occur at 420 nm. Recently, 2DM-dielectric multilayers have been
proposed as candidates for HMMs. The HMM makes it possible to
incorporate an ultrathin super-graphene-based absorber into detectors
and thereby enhance light absorption [53]. Chang et al. reported the
numerical and experimental realization of an HMM structure of multi-
layer alternating graphene-    pairs [54]. A biaxial HMM consisting
of multilayer alternating BP-gold in the range of 400-900 nm was pro-
posed and discussed by Song et al. [55]. However, to our knowledge, no
one has extensively researched the design of an HMM broadband
absorber utilizing BP.

based on BP HMMs, which is composed of multilayer alternating BP/
dielectric structures stacked on an iron mirror. Compared with previous
studies, the proposed HMM provided previously unattained hyperbolic

structural parameters within a specified range. Next, the FDTD method

fitness function, and the optimization process ended when the maximum
number of iterations was reached or the optimal average absorption was
satisfied. If it wasn't, the particles would update their positions ac-
cording to the fitness function. The proposed optimization process was
then repeated until the standard stopped. More information on PSO can

physical concepts between the VPPs and LSPR are different. Surface
plasmon polaritons (SPPs) are transmission dispersive electromagnetic
waves coupled with the electron plasma of the conductor at the interface
of the medium [77]. The volume plasmon polariton is formed by the
coupling of short-range surface plasmon polariton excitations in the
individual metal layers. So various groups call them multilayer plas-
mons, or VPPs [48]. Local-SPPs (LSPs) are excited by conduction band
electrons in metal nanostructures coupled with electromagnetic fields.

Conclusion
In conclusion, we have proposed a planar HMM structure utilizing
anisotropic monolayer BP and demonstrated the hyperbolic character-
istic of the proposed structure. The HMM's permittivity differed signif-
icantly from that of pristine BP due to the filling ratio of BP. We utilized a
PSO algorithm to optimize the structure of the HMM and confirmed the
optimal structure was a sawtooth slab, which excited the local and
volume plasmon modes of both HMMs. Numerical results showed that
the average absorption was up to 93.4% from 30 to    (3.3 to 10
THz), and the absorption was omnidirectional and polarization sensi-
tive. These results show that our proposed HMM absorber could easily
be applied in the IR to THz region, and this study provides practical
guidelines for future research on biaxial HMMs and anisotropic 2DMs.
