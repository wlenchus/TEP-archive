## **1. Introduction: The Epistemic Pivot and the Crisis of Bulk Ontology**

The current trajectory of theoretical physics is defined by a persistent and increasingly acute tension between the deterministic geometry of General Relativity and the unitary probabilistic framework of Quantum Mechanics. While this approach has yielded the Standard Model, it faces intractable difficulties at the interfaces of horizons, singularities, and measurement limits, most notably in the Black Hole Information Paradox and the fine-tuning problems of cosmology.

The framework under review, designated the Terminal Equivalence Principle (TEP), proposes a radical methodological correction to this impasse. The derivation of gravity remains the framework's weakest link.
