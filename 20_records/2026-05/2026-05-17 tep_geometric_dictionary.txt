# A TEP Geometric Dictionary for the Riemann ζ-Field

**Companion note to "A Dyadic Cascade Representation of the Riemann ξ
Function with Renormalization-Group Structure"**

**Working draft, May 2026**

## Abstract

We document an internally consistent geometric translation between the
structural features of the Riemann ζ-field on the (σ, t)-plane and the
rapidity-budget machinery of the Terminal Equivalence Principle (TEP).
The translation identifies σ as a memory-weighting axis, t as an
associativity-degree axis, the strip orientation θ_strip as a TEP state
angle satisfying x_strip = sin(θ_strip), and the discrete-vs-continuous
coarse-graining unit dl as a phase-space probe scale whose σ- and
t-resolutions decompose under Heisenberg-style conjugacy controlled by
θ_strip. The cascade rapidity α\_∞(s) = 2 log ζ(s/2) − log ζ(s) admits a
logarithmic singularity at each Riemann zero, with operational
regularity off-zero: matched-filter probes resolve α\_∞ to fixed
relative accuracy on truncated domains (excluding δ_0-neighborhoods of
zeros) with chirp-duration scaling as log log(1/δ_0). Riemann's argument
principle becomes a TEP conservation law: the integer count of enclosed
zeros is invariant under continuous deformation of the integration
contour including rotation through θ_strip --- structurally analogous to
a Lorentz-invariant scalar preserved across boost frames, with the
cascade's passive-rapidity (η) and reactive-phase (φ) components
transforming among themselves as harmonic conjugates. The dictionary is
interpretive --- it does not produce new theorems about ζ or constrain
zero locations --- but it provides a coherent framework-internal reading
of how ζ's standard duality (primes ↔ zeros, sum ↔ integral)
instantiates TEP's machinery, with quantitative operational thresholds
for off-zero analysis.

## 1. Introduction and scope

The companion paper establishes a dyadic cascade representation of the
Riemann ξ function, with explicit prime + elementary decomposition and
quantitative renormalized convergence. That paper is structural
mathematics, and its results stand independently. This note serves a
different purpose: it documents the *interpretive correspondence*
between the geometric features of ζ's complex domain and TEP's
rapidity-budget framework, with appropriate care about what constitutes
proven content versus heuristic translation.

We claim no new mathematics about ζ here. The argument principle (§4)
and the cascade rapidity decomposition (§3) are standard. What is new is
the *assembly*: the demonstration that TEP's three-term budget x² + w +
u = 1, the SU(1,1) cascade machinery, and the discrete/continuous
Fourier duality between primes and the imaginary line align coherently
with a single geometric dictionary on the (σ, t)-plane. Whether such a
dictionary is *useful* is a separate question; this note demonstrates
that it is at least *consistent*.

## 2. Coordinate identification

We adopt the identification

> σ ↔ memory weight (temporal coordinate),\
> t ↔ phase rotation (spatial coordinate)

consistently throughout. The motivation: σ controls the magnitude
weighting of cascade rungs $r_k = p_k^{-s/2}$ via
$|r_k| = p_k^{-\sigma/2}$. As σ varies, the privilege of early primes
over late primes shifts. Higher σ → strong early-prime privilege
(forward arrow of cascade-time, dissipative). Lower σ → flatter
weighting (slower decay, more democratic). σ = 1/2 is the *minimum-bias*
line --- the slowest decay rate at which $r_k \to 0$ still holds, the
line where no directional bias on early-vs-late primes is imposed by the
magnitude decay.

The phase rotation, $\arg(r_k) = -(t/2)\log p_k$, controls *where in the
prime cycle* each rung sits. As t varies along the strip, the cumulative
phase $\Phi_N(t) = -(t/2)\sum_{k\leq N}\log p_k$ traces a trajectory on
the unit circle. This is positional structure in cascade-space.

A critical refinement: every vertical strip carries memory. The σ = 1/2
line is not "memory-free" --- it is the line of minimum
memory-asymmetry. Memory itself is everywhere except along the t = 0
real axis, where the cascade Möbius transformations become real scalars
and commute (associative limit, see §5).

## 3. The cascade rapidity decomposition

Define the cascade rapidity

> α\_∞(s) := log A(s), A(s) := ζ(s/2)² / ζ(s), (1)

a meromorphic function on ℂ with branch points at zeros and poles of
ζ(s/2) and ζ(s). Decompose into real and imaginary parts:

> α\_∞(s) = η(s) + i φ(s) (2)

with

> η(s) = log\|A(s)\| (passive rapidity, magnitude),\
> φ(s) = arg A(s) (reactive phase, modulo 2π).

Since α\_∞ is holomorphic on its domain of regularity, the
Cauchy--Riemann equations imply η and φ are harmonic conjugates:

> ∂η/∂σ = ∂φ/∂t,\
> ∂η/∂t = −∂φ/∂σ. (3)

This is the analytic-function instance of the boost-rotation duality: η
and φ form an SO(2)-rotation-equivariant pair under deformations in the
(σ, t)-plane.

The reactive phase φ(s) is naturally an arctan ratio:

> φ(s) = 2 arg ζ(s/2) − arg ζ(s) = 2 arctan\[Im ζ(s/2) / Re ζ(s/2)\] −
> arctan\[Im ζ(s) / Re ζ(s)\] (4)

(modulo π wrap-around at branch crossings). This explicit arctan-ratio
form was hinted at in the framework's early notes; (4) makes it precise.

Riemann zeros register as π-jumps in φ. On the critical line, arg ζ(½ +
it) jumps by π at each γ_n, while arg ζ(¼ + it/2) varies smoothly. The
zero structure of ζ thus appears as discrete topological features in the
*reactive phase component* of the cascade rapidity, not as anomalies in
the *passive magnitude component*. This is consistent with --- and
explains --- the falsified "constructive accumulation in deficit
consumption" intuition: the zeros are phase-coordinate features, not
magnitude-coordinate features at finite truncation.

## 4. Strip integrations and the conservation law

For a closed contour ∂R in the (σ, t)-plane bounding a region R that
avoids the critical singular set,

> ∮*{∂R} dα*∞(s) = 2πi · \[2 N\_{ζ(s/2)}(R) − N\_{ζ(s)}(R) − 2
> P\_{ζ(s/2)}(R) + P\_{ζ(s)}(R)\] (5)

where N_f(R) counts zeros of f inside R and P_f(R) counts poles. This is
the argument principle applied to A(s) = ζ(s/2)²/ζ(s); we record it here
in TEP-friendly notation.

Equation (5) is a **conservation law** in two senses: (a) the integral
is invariant under continuous deformation of ∂R within an analytic
region (no singularities crossed), and (b) the integral is quantized in
units of 2πi (topological invariant). In TEP rapidity language: the
total cascade boost around any closed loop equals an integer multiple of
2π, weighted by zero/pole counts at the natural scales s and s/2.

For an ε-width vertical strip
$[\sigma_0 − \varepsilon/2, \sigma_0 + \varepsilon/2] \times [t_1, t_2]$,
the boundary integral encodes the count of zeros (at scale s and s/2)
crossed in the t-range, independent of ε. As ε → 0, the strip
degenerates to a vertical line segment, and the integral approaches a
sum of π-jumps at zeros lying on σ = σ_0. The topological count is
preserved.

For an ε-width horizontal strip
$[\sigma_1, \sigma_2] \times [t_0 − \varepsilon/2, t_0 + \varepsilon/2]$,
the boundary integral encodes the magnitude profile of A(s) along the
σ-direction at near-fixed t_0, plus boundary-jump contributions if t_0
is at a zero. As ε → 0, the strip degenerates to a horizontal line
segment, and the integral approaches the smooth η-difference η(σ_2, t_0)
− η(σ_1, t_0).

## 5. The strip dictionary

We now state the geometric dictionary in tabular form. Two distinct
gradient parameters control the cascade's character along each axis:

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  Axis                    Parameter               What varies
  ----------------------- ----------------------- -----------------------
  σ-direction             memory weight asymmetry early-vs-late prime
                                                  privilege;
                                                  arrow-of-cascade-time
                                                  strength

  t-direction             associativity degree    commutator scaling
                                                  \[f_a, f_b\] of Möbius
                                                  rungs (0 at t = 0, O(t)
                                                  small, O(1) large)
  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

The σ = 1/2 line is the *symmetric memory-weighting* line --- minimum
directional bias on early-vs-late primes --- but it is *not*
memory-free. The t = 0 line is the *associative* line --- Möbius rungs
are real and commute, so prime ordering is genuinely irrelevant. These
are different structural conditions on the cascade. They cross at the
single point s = 1/2 ∈ ℝ, which has no special analytic significance for
ζ (ζ(1/2) ≈ −1.46).

**Strip orientation as TEP state angle.** Define θ_strip ∈ \[0, π\] as
the angle of the strip's long axis from the σ-direction. Then:

  -------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  θ_strip        strip type     x_strip = sin  u_strip = cos² character
                                θ_strip        θ_strip        
  -------------- -------------- -------------- -------------- -------------------
  0, π           horizontal     0              1              discrete summation
                 (long axis                                   over primes; budget
                 along σ)                                     pure-absorption

  π/2            vertical (long 1              0              continuous
                 axis along t)                                integration over
                                                              phase; budget
                                                              pure-transmission

  intermediate   diagonal       sin θ          cos² θ         mixed budget
                                                              partition
  -------------------------------------------------------------------------------

This identification places the strip orientation directly inside TEP's
three-term budget: a horizontal strip realizes the perfectly destructive
limit (u = 1, all energy absorbed into the cascade, no boundary
transmission), a vertical strip realizes the boundary-hit limit (x = 1,
no absorption, full transmission), and intermediate strips realize mixed
states with budget partitioned by sin² θ + cos² θ = 1.

**Coarse-graining unit dl.** Distinct from the orientation θ_strip, dl
characterizes the *aggregation mode* along the strip:

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  dl regime               operation               along which axis
                                                  natively
  ----------------------- ----------------------- -----------------------
  dl → 0                  continuous integration  t-direction (continuous
                          (∫)                     imaginary line)

  dl → 1                  discrete summation (Σ)  σ-direction (primes are
                                                  discrete)
  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

This reflects an intrinsic asymmetry of the ζ-field: the σ-direction has
discrete structure built in (the primes are indexed $p_1, p_2, \ldots$,
with σ controlling their relative weights), while the t-direction is
purely continuous (no preferred sampling along the imaginary axis).
Operations along σ are naturally Σ-type; operations along t are
naturally ∫-type.

The interplay between θ_strip and dl realizes a Fourier-uncertainty
trade-off: a strip's "sampling rate" along its long axis trades against
its "window width" perpendicular to it. Pure horizontal sampling has
high σ-sampling rate (one prime per dl-step) but smears the t-coordinate
over the ε-window. Pure vertical sampling has high t-sampling rate
(continuous) but selects a single σ-value within the ε-window. Diagonal
strips interpolate.

**Positional weighting.** Two basis-aligned strips at the same
orientation but different positions encode different cascade behaviors:

-   Vertical strip at σ_0 = 1/2 (critical-line strip): forced-order
    cascade with symmetric memory weighting; this is the locus of all
    nontrivial ζ zeros.
-   Vertical strip at σ_0 = 1.3: forced-order cascade with strong
    early-prime privilege; convergent prime sum, no zeros (Hadamard--de
    la Vallée Poussin).
-   Horizontal strip at t_0 = γ_n: integration over σ at the level of a
    Riemann zero; boundary-jump contributions register the zero.
-   Horizontal strip at t_0 = 10 (off-zero): smooth integration over σ;
    no jump contributions.

The arrow-of-direction surfaces here. σ \> 1/2 and σ \< 1/2 are
FE-related by σ ↔ 1 − σ; the σ \> 1/2 side is "dissipative" (early
primes dominate, late-prime contribution suppressed), and σ \< 1/2 is
"anti-dissipative" (formally --- the cascade leaves the strict-passive
regime, but the metric-budget identity persists with appropriate
signature). The functional equation maps these as
time-reversal-conjugate frames. Similarly, +t and −t produce cascades
with opposite chirality of phase accumulation: the cumulative phase
$\Phi_N(t) = -(t/2)\sum\log p_k$ reverses sign, and the regions of the
prime list that experience constructive interference shift to
complementary subsets. This is structurally the cascade analog of the
integer Dirichlet series weight-flip across Re(s) = 1, but operating
through phase rather than through magnitude.

## 5.5. Coarse-graining: how dl interpolates with strip orientation

The coarse-graining unit dl introduced in §5 has clean operational
meaning at the boundary cases (Σ-step along σ, dt along t) but requires
unpacking at intermediate strip angles. The right framing is as a
phase-space probe scale, with the strip orientation θ_strip controlling
how the probe's resolution decomposes between σ and t directions.

### 5.5.1. Phase-space probe interpretation

A strip at angle θ_strip with center $(\sigma_0, t_0)$ and ε-width
perpendicular to its long axis can be read as a windowed probe of the
cascade observable. The probe's σ- and t-resolutions are not
independent: they are linked by Heisenberg-style conjugacy, with the
link mediated by the strip orientation.

For a probe of long-axis length scale $dl$, the resolutions in the (σ,
t)-coordinates are

$$\Delta\sigma_\theta^2 = dl^2 \cos^2\theta + \frac{1}{dl^2}\sin^2\theta, \qquad \Delta t_\theta^2 = dl^2 \sin^2\theta + \frac{1}{dl^2}\cos^2\theta. \quad (6)$$

These satisfy $\Delta\sigma_\theta\,\Delta t_\theta \geq 1/2$ with
equality iff $dl = 1$ (the saturation locus) for any θ, or iff θ ∈ {0,
π/2} (the axis-aligned cases).

At the boundary cases:

-   **θ = 0 (horizontal):** $\Delta\sigma_\theta = dl$,
    $\Delta t_\theta = 1/dl$. The σ-resolution *is* dl; the t-window is
    its conjugate $1/dl$. Sampling along σ at step dl smears across t at
    scale $1/dl$.
-   **θ = π/2 (vertical):** $\Delta\sigma_\theta = 1/dl$,
    $\Delta t_\theta = dl$. The roles swap. The t-resolution is dl; the
    σ-window is $1/dl$.

At intermediate θ, dl is a coupled phase-space scale that contributes to
both σ and t resolutions weighted by $(\cos\theta, \sin\theta)$. The
same physical "step size" along the strip's long axis manifests as a
σ-step or a t-step depending on orientation, with the conjugate variable
picking up the remainder.

### 5.5.2. Prime-density modulation

The natural σ-step is not uniform across the σ-axis. By the Prime Number
Theorem, the average gap between successive primes near $p$ is $\log p$,
so the natural σ-step in *prime-natural* units at scale $p$ is
$\Delta\ell_\sigma \sim 1/\log p$. The Heisenberg saturation locus
$dl = 1$ is therefore in *absolute* (σ, t)-units, but in prime-natural
units the same condition becomes $dl = \log p$, scale-dependent.

The operational consequence: probes of the cascade at scale
corresponding to prime $p_k$ have natural σ-resolution $1/\log p_k$, and
the conjugate t-window for matched-filter probes is $\log p_k$. As
$p_k \to \infty$, the σ-resolution improves while the t-window grows ---
the cascade "self-refines" at higher prime scales, which is the
operational form of the framework's earlier observation that prime
sampling becomes finer as one moves to larger primes.

### 5.5.3. Chirp and pulse coherence

The Fourier-conjugacy reading of (6) admits a sharper interpretation
through the chirp/pulse picture. A strip at angle θ_strip realizes a
probe whose long-axis "duration" is independent of its "sweep
direction":

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  Probe character                     Strip realization
  ----------------------------------- -----------------------------------
  **Long-duration chirp, slow sweep** long L, intermediate θ ---
                                      distinguishably swept across both σ
                                      and t; high temporal coherence
                                      (fine ordering of contributions),
                                      low spatial coherence (frequencies
                                      smeared together)

  **Short pulse**                     short L, any θ --- many frequencies
                                      arrive simultaneously; low temporal
                                      coherence, high spatial coherence

  **Pure σ-sweep**                    long L, θ = 0 --- discrete
                                      prime-by-prime accumulation;
                                      horizontal strip in the geometric
                                      dictionary

  **Pure t-sweep**                    long L, θ = π/2 --- continuous
                                      phase accumulation; vertical strip
                                      in the geometric dictionary
  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

This recasts the geometric dictionary's strip-orientation parameter as a
Fourier-conjugacy parameter on the same phase-space, with the intrinsic
discrete-vs-continuous asymmetry of the ζ-field (primes discrete on σ,
Lebesgue continuous on t) providing a fixed reference for what "sweep"
and "duration" mean operationally.

### 5.5.4. Poisson-CDF reconstruction threshold

A concrete realization of the chirp-duration / event-count threshold is
provided by the elementary identity

$$x \cdot \frac{\Gamma(1+k, \log x)}{k!} \to x \quad \text{as } k \to \infty, \quad (7)$$

where $\Gamma(1+k, \cdot)$ is the upper incomplete gamma function. The
ratio $\Gamma(1+k, \log x)/k!$ is the cumulative distribution function
of a Poisson($\log x$) random variable evaluated at $k$:

$$\frac{\Gamma(1+k, \log x)}{k!} = e^{-\log x}\sum_{j=0}^{k}\frac{(\log x)^j}{j!} = \mathbb{P}\big[\text{Poisson}(\log x) \leq k\big]. \quad (8)$$

For $x \gg 1$ (so $\log x > 0$), the saturation threshold is
$k \sim \log x$: at $k \ll \log x$ the CDF is small, at
$k \approx \log x$ it is at the half-mass point, and at $k \gg \log x$
it converges super-exponentially to 1.

The chirp-duration reading: $k$ is the "duration" of the reconstruction
(number of accumulated Poisson events), and $\log x$ is the natural
event-scale at the value $x$. Faithful reconstruction requires duration
to substantially exceed the event-scale. In the framework's strip
language, this gives a concrete operational threshold for the long-axis
length L of a strip that resolves cascade observables at scale
$|f| \sim x$:

$$L_{\text{min}}(x) \sim \log x. \quad (9)$$

For prime-supported observables at scale $p$, $\log x \sim \log p$
matches the prime-density modulation of §5.5.2: the same $\log p$ factor
appears as both the "natural σ-step" and the "minimum strip duration for
reconstruction." The two readings are operationally consistent.

## 5.6. Operational regularity of cascade rapidity off-zero

The cascade rapidity $\alpha_\infty(s) = 2\log\zeta(s/2) - \log\zeta(s)$
has a logarithmic singularity at each Riemann zero $\rho_n$:

$$|\alpha_\infty(s)| \sim \log|s - \rho_n|^{-1} \quad \text{as } s \to \rho_n. \quad (10)$$

The singularity is *soft* --- logarithmic rather than polynomial --- but
it is a genuine divergence, not a removable feature.

### 5.6.1. Uniform off-zero bound

For any $\delta_0 > 0$, on any compact region K of the (σ, t)-plane, and
on the truncated domain $K \setminus \bigcup_n B(\rho_n, \delta_0)$
(compact set with $\delta_0$-neighborhoods of zeros excluded),
$\alpha_\infty$ is uniformly bounded:

$$\sup_{s \in K \setminus \bigcup_n B(\rho_n, \delta_0)} |\alpha_\infty(s)| \;\leq\; \log(1/\delta_0) + C(K), \quad (11)$$

where $C(K)$ depends only on the compact region K (collecting
contributions from regular parts of $\zeta$ and $\zeta(s/2)$ over K,
plus their pole structure).

This uniform bound is a *truncated-domain* statement: it holds away from
zeros but does not extend across them. Within the excluded
neighborhoods, $\alpha_\infty$ exhibits its full logarithmic
singularity.

### 5.6.2. Matched-filter resolution off-zero

The Poisson-CDF reconstruction
$\mathcal{P}(\alpha_\infty; k, k) := \alpha_\infty \cdot \Gamma(1+k, \log\alpha_\infty)/k!$
resolves $\alpha_\infty$ to fixed *relative* accuracy on the truncated
domain, with truncation depth scaling doubly-logarithmically:

$$k \gtrsim \log\log(1/\delta_0) + \log(1/\varepsilon) \implies \frac{|\mathcal{P}_k(\alpha_\infty) - \alpha_\infty|}{|\alpha_\infty|} \leq \varepsilon \quad \text{on } K \setminus \bigcup_n B(\rho_n, \delta_0). \quad (12)$$

This is operational regularity off-zero, mediated by the matched-filter
probe. Its honest scope:

-   **What it gives:** uniform *relative* accuracy on the truncated
    domain with chirp-duration scaling as $\log\log(1/\delta_0)$ ---
    exponentially better than the bare-ζ scaling $\log(1/\delta_0)$.
-   **What it does not give:** a uniform *absolute* bound. The residual
    $|\mathcal{P}_k - \alpha_\infty|$ at fixed $k$ grows with
    $|\alpha_\infty|$ as $s$ approaches a zero; relative accuracy is
    preserved, but absolute closeness is not. Matched-filter probes
    provide *subordination* of the divergence at constant relative
    scale, not *domination* in the absolute sense.

### 5.6.3. The boundary obstruction

The framework's earlier intuition --- that the cascade's hitting the
unit-disc boundary $|x_\infty| = 1$ might be a finite-N detectable
signature of zeros --- was falsified at the level of finite-N cascade
dynamics (cf. project status summary, R.1--R.5). A clarifying
reformulation:

The three statements

(i) $\zeta(s) = 0$,\
(ii) $A(s) := \zeta(s/2)^2/\zeta(s) = \infty$,\
(iii) $|x_\infty(s)| = 1$ at the +1 boundary

are equivalent under the cascade map. Approaching the boundary along the
σ-direction from $\rho_1$:

  σ-distance   $|x_\infty|$   $1 - |x_\infty|$       $|1/\alpha_\infty|$
  ------------ -------------- ---------------------- ----------------------
  $10^{-1}$    0.9088         $9.1 \times 10^{-2}$   $3.5 \times 10^{-1}$
  $10^{-3}$    0.99902        $9.8 \times 10^{-4}$   $1.3 \times 10^{-1}$
  $10^{-5}$    0.99999        $9.8 \times 10^{-6}$   $8.3 \times 10^{-2}$
  $10^{-7}$    0.9999999      $9.8 \times 10^{-8}$   $6.0 \times 10^{-2}$

The boundary-distance $1 - |x_\infty|$ vanishes as $\delta^2$
(polynomially), while $|1/\alpha_\infty|$ vanishes only as
$1/\log(1/\delta)$ (logarithmically). Inverting to $1/\alpha_\infty$
converts the cascade rapidity divergence into bounded zeros at $\rho_n$,
but with logarithmic vanishing rate --- substantially slower than the
boundary-distance.

The structural point: $1/\alpha_\infty$ is bounded near zeros but
encodes the same zero-information set as $\zeta$. Specifically, its
zeros are exactly:

-   $\rho_n$ (Riemann zeros, where $A \to \infty$,
    $\alpha_\infty \to \infty$)
-   $2\rho_n$ (zeros of $\zeta(s/2)$, where $A \to 0$,
    $\alpha_\infty \to -\infty$)

The inversion trades one form of singularity for another --- logarithmic
divergences become logarithmic-order zeros --- but does not eliminate
zero-information. The *type* of singularity changes; its presence does
not.

This is the genuine boundary obstruction: any holomorphic transform of
$\alpha_\infty$ that is bounded everywhere must either (a) preserve all
zero-information of ζ in some encoding, or (b) lose the connection to ζ
entirely. There is no holomorphic regularization that cancels
divergences at zeros while preserving their location. The cascade
boundary in $x_\infty$-coordinates is the image of the ζ-zero set under
a fixed analytic map; reaching the boundary *is* having a zero;
relabeling does not constrain where zeros are.

### 5.6.4. Operational summary

The cascade rapidity is the natural framework observable for off-zero
analysis:

-   **Off-zero (truncated domain $\delta \geq \delta_0$):**
    $|\alpha_\infty|$ uniformly bounded by $\log(1/\delta_0)$;
    matched-filter probes of duration $k \sim \log\log(1/\delta_0)$
    resolve $\alpha_\infty$ to fixed relative accuracy.
-   **At zeros ($\delta = 0$):** $|\alpha_\infty|$ diverges
    logarithmically; no finite-duration probe resolves the divergence;
    inversion to $1/\alpha_\infty$ produces logarithmic-order zeros that
    retain full zero-information.

The framework's value is in operational efficiency on truncated domains,
not in domination of zero-locations. The TEP-passive language records
the cascade boundary as the ζ-zero locus in a structurally clean form;
it does not constrain where on the critical strip those zeros lie. The
off-zero operational regularity (12) is a genuine reduction from the
bare-ζ scaling --- useful in computational practice and in
framework-internal arguments --- but does not extend across zeros.

## 6. Conservation under TEP boost

Combining (3), (5), and the strip-as-state-angle dictionary: the
topological zero count enclosed in a region R is invariant under
continuous rotation of the bounding strip's orientation θ_strip. The (η,
φ) decomposition of α\_∞ transforms among itself under such rotation ---
the harmonic-conjugate equations (3) generate an SO(2) action on (η, φ)
at each (σ, t) --- but the integer count is preserved.

This is structurally identical to the relativistic statement that
(energy, momentum) transform among themselves under Lorentz boost, while
the invariant mass m² = E² − p² is preserved. In our context:

-   (η, φ) plays the role of (energy, momentum),
-   θ_strip plays the role of the boost angle,
-   the integer zero count plays the role of invariant mass,
-   the conservation law (5) plays the role of mass-shell invariance.

The disanalogy with strict relativity: SO(2) here is Euclidean rotation,
not SO(1,1) hyperbolic boost. The cascade target space (η, φ ∈ ℝ ×
(ℝ/2π)) is naturally cylindrical because φ is multi-valued modulo 2π,
and the metric on this cylinder is Euclidean. So we have a *compactified
rapidity space* --- the natural target for a passive cascade --- rather
than a Minkowski rapidity space. This is consistent with TEP's
passive-rung structure (boundary-bounded, not boundary-blowup) rather
than relativistic free-particle structure.

## 7. The Markovian/forced-order dichotomy

The geometric dictionary makes the classical PNT-vs-RH structural
distinction TEP-natural:

**Markovian (associative) regime --- t = 0 line:** Möbius rungs are real
scalars, all commute. Order of primes is irrelevant. Cascade is fully
Markovian. ζ is positive on this line (for σ \> 1, by Euler product;
analytic continuation gives smooth values elsewhere on the real axis
with the pole at σ = 1 and trivial zeros at σ = -2, -4, ...). No
nontrivial zeros on the real axis.

**Forced-order regime --- t ≠ 0:** Möbius rungs are complex; commutator
\[f_a, f_b\] = O(t) at small t, O(1) at large t. Cascade is
non-Markovian; prime ordering is structurally enforced.

**Symmetric weighting --- σ = 1/2 line:** minimum directional bias of
memory weighting. The line on which all known nontrivial zeros lie.

**RH in this language:** the only nontrivial zeros of ζ occur where
forced-order regime (t ≠ 0) meets symmetric weighting (σ = 1/2). The two
structural conditions are individually loose --- forced-order holds for
any t ≠ 0, symmetric weighting holds along an entire vertical line ---
but their *zero-bearing intersection* is conjecturally a single line.
This is RH expressed as a TEP-purity statement: zero-bearing is
precisely the regime of maximal symmetric forced-order, which is
conjecturally a one-dimensional locus.

This restatement does not advance the proof of RH. It does locate the
framework's natural reading of why σ = 1/2 is the "right" line: it is
the unique line of symmetric memory weighting, and zeros (by RH) are
conjectured to lie there because the symmetric-weighting condition
combines with forced-order to single out a one-parameter family.

## 9. Conclusion

The Riemann ζ-field admits a coherent TEP-rapidity-budget interpretation
in which:

-   σ encodes memory weighting and t encodes associativity degree;
-   strip orientation θ_strip is a TEP state angle, sin θ_strip =
    x_strip;
-   the coarse-graining unit dl is a phase-space probe scale whose σ-
    and t-resolutions decompose under Heisenberg-style conjugacy
    controlled by θ_strip, with prime-density modulation at scale $p$
    giving natural step $1/\log p$ and conjugate window $\log p$;
-   the cascade rapidity decomposes into harmonic-conjugate (η, φ) ---
    passive magnitude and reactive phase;
-   Riemann zeros register as π-jumps in φ, not as features in η;
-   $\alpha_\infty$ has logarithmic singularities at zeros but is
    uniformly bounded off-zero, with matched-filter probes resolving it
    to fixed relative accuracy at chirp-duration $\log\log(1/\delta_0)$
    --- exponentially better than bare ζ;
-   the cascade-boundary $|x_\infty| = 1$ is the image of the ζ-zero set
    under the cascade map; reaching the boundary *is* having a zero;
    inversion to $1/\alpha_\infty$ relabels the singularity type but
    preserves the zero-information;
-   the argument principle is a conservation law preserving the
    topological zero count under continuous deformation including
    rotation through θ_strip;
-   the (η, φ) decomposition transforms among itself under such
    rotation, with the integer zero count as boost-invariant scalar;
-   RH is the TEP-purity statement that zero-bearing is precisely the
    locus where forced-order meets symmetric-weighting --- a single line
    by conjecture.

This is interpretive geometry with operational content, not new
mathematics about ζ-zero locations. The dictionary makes the framework's
structural intuitions precise and portable, makes contact with standard
results (argument principle, explicit formula, FE) at every step, and
provides genuine quantitative reductions from bare-ζ analysis on
truncated domains. It does not advance RH or provide new bounds on zero
locations.

We recommend this note be paired with the dyadic cascade paper as a
companion document, with both framed as structural contributions: the
dyadic paper as a multi-scale exact representation of ξ with
quantitative renormalized convergence, and this note as the geometric
dictionary that situates that representation within TEP's
rapidity-budget framework, with operational thresholds for off-zero
analysis.
