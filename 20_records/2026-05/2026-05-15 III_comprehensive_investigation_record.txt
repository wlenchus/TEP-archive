## Part 9: Methodological Lapses and Corrections

Several methodological corrections occurred during this section of the
investigation. Documenting them is part of the discipline that makes the
framework's content credible.

### The DHO identification mistake

I initially pattern-matched the framework's involution form to the DHO's
$\omega_d/\omega_n = \sqrt{1-\zeta^2}$ relation and treated
$\omega_d/\omega_n$ as the signal variable. The corpus actually uses

Lesson: when the framework's form appears in a physical sector, check
the *corpus identification* rather than picking the identification that
makes the form look right. The form being right is necessary but not
sufficient.

### The reactive-crossing oversight

After the first correction, I assumed I had the relevant DHO
identification. The user then pointed to a *different* corpus
identification at a different cascade-family level: the
reactive-crossing $x_z^2 = p/(1+p)$ with $p = \zeta_1\zeta_2$. This was
the substantively interesting case I had missed.

Lesson: "the relevant identification" isn't unique. The same physical
system supports multiple framework instantiations simultaneously, each
operationally meaningful. Asking which identification is relevant
requires asking which *observable* we're considering.

### The over-retreat on derivational status

In the TIR/Goos-Hänchen analysis, I framed the framework's content as
"structural reorganization rather than first-principles derivation,"
treating it as Tier 2. The user pushed back: this conflated gauge
specification with derivational content. The framework derives
gauge-independent structural physics; specific dimensional content is
separately specified empirically. This is real derivation, not
structural reorganization.

Lesson: the appropriate standard for the framework is
*gauge-independent* derivation, not *specific-dimensional* derivation.
The two are distinct, and conflating them was the error. I had been
applying a strict "derivation = specific equations" standard to TIR
while having accepted "derivation = structural form" for Haldane ---
inconsistent application of standards.

### The imaginary-G over-reach

In the earlier imaginary-G investigation, I had framed the unification
of tunneling, TIR, overdamping, Wick rotation, band gaps, Hawking-Unruh
as instances of the framework's $T$-cycle operation. This was
over-reach.

Honest reading: these phenomena share an algebraic shape (analytic
continuation past critical thresholds, with imaginary continuation
giving evanescent observables). The framework's apparatus correctly
identifies and organizes this algebraic shape. But "share an algebraic
shape and are cleanly described by the framework's variables" is not
"unified as instances of a single derivation." Each phenomenon has its
own underlying physics; the framework reorganizes the post-critical
observables of each but doesn't replace the underlying equations.

The corrected reading: the framework's universal claim covers the
*structural form* of post-critical observables, not their derivation
from underlying microscopic theory. This is the appropriate scope.

### Two distinct error directions

The investigation produced both *over-claiming* errors
(synthesis-document framings, the imaginary-G unification reach) and
*under-claiming* errors (the TIR Tier-2 retreat). Both are failures of
calibration; the discipline requires correcting each direction with
